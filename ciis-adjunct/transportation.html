Getting here

1453 Mission Street is well served by BART, Muni, and bike lanes in the Civic Center / SoMa neighborhood.

Address: 1453 Mission Street, San Francisco, CA 94103 — close to City Hall and the Civic Center BART station.

Public transit

CIIS is one of the best-connected campuses in San Francisco for public transit. Parking is expensive and limited, so transit is strongly encouraged.

BART

Closest station: Civic Center / UN Plaza — about a 5–7 minute walk.

  • Connects to the East Bay, Peninsula (including SFO), and South Bay
  • Exit at Civic Center / UN Plaza, then walk south toward Mission St
bart.gov

Muni

Multiple Muni lines stop within a block or two on Mission Street and nearby corridors.

  • Metro lines J, K, L, M, N, T stop at Civic Center station
  • Bus lines include the 14 (Mission) and 49 (Van Ness/Mission)
  • Clipper transfers between BART and Muni are discounted within 90 minutes
sfmta.com

Biking

Bike parking

Secure bike parking is available for faculty and students.

  • Public racks on the street outside 1453 Mission St
  • A designated building bike storage area — ask the front desk for access

Always use a sturdy U-lock, even in the building's storage area.

Bay Wheels (bike share)

Stations are located near Civic Center and along Market Street, with electric and standard bikes.

Protected bike lanes run nearby, including on Valencia Street, one block east of Mission.

baywheels.com

Parking

A word on driving

The Civic Center / SoMa neighborhood is not known for easy or affordable parking. If you must drive, allow extra time and budget for parking costs.

Nearby garages

  • Civic Center Garage — 355 McAllister St (~5 min walk)
  • 1355 Market Street Garage (~7 min walk)
  • Check SpotHero or ParkWhiz for current rates and availability

Street parking

  • Metered hours are typically 9am–6pm on weekdays; some meters run until 10pm
  • Evening and weekend parking is often more available
  • Pre-book garage spots through SpotHero or ParkWhiz for discounts

Rideshare & other options

Rideshare

Lyft and Uber serve the area. Drop-off on Mission St is usually straightforward; expect surge pricing at peak times.

Walking

From SoMa, the Mission, Hayes Valley, or the Castro, CIIS is potentially walkable. The Civic Center area is flat and pedestrian-friendly.

Scooters

Shared electric scooters are frequently available near Civic Center. Check scooter apps for docking locations.

A note on the neighborhood

Civic Center / SoMa is a vibrant, complex neighborhood with a significant unhoused population, particularly around UN Plaza. CIIS's social justice commitments include approaching our neighbors with dignity and awareness. If you have concerns about your commute, especially for evening classes, ask the program coordinator about available resources.
