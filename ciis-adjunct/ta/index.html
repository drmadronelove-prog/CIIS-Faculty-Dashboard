Teaching assistant orientation

As an ICP teaching assistant, your work spans advising, practicum support, advocacy, and administration. Here's how each area works.

General advising

  • Support first-semester students with class flow questions and navigating the stress of being a new student.
  • Send a proactive email or newsletter each semester with important dates (add/drop deadlines) and your office hours.
  • Try to check in with all advisees at least once per semester.
  • Limit meetings to 15 minutes.
  • Do not take on more than 40 advisees.

Practicum support

  • Hold pre-practicum conversations: discuss therapy styles and possible clinic site placement.
  • Check students' unofficial transcripts in Colleague to confirm all pre-practicum courses are passed — no incompletes (I/IP) and no failed courses that haven't been retaken.
  • Require students to meet with you in person to go over their practicum application — do not just sign it.
  • Review clinical evaluation forms before the meeting to identify themes.
  • In the meeting, discuss readiness, fears, and anxieties about starting practicum.

Problem solving and advocacy

  • First step: encourage the student to speak directly with the instructor.
  • Facilitate a conversation between student and instructor if needed.
  • Title IX situations: notify the program chairs immediately.
  • Advocate for students on other issues — independent study, tuition reimbursement, waitlist anxiety, and more.
  • Goal: keep everyone on track to graduate on time.

Administrative

Sign forms as needed: registration forms, change of advisor forms, fieldwork agreement forms, and others.
