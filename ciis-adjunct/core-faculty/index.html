Advising

Each ICP student is automatically assigned a faculty academic advisor. Advisors offer personal and professional support — orienting students to university processes, connecting them to resources, mentoring, and advocating (including on Title IX and grievance matters). Students are encouraged to meet their advisor at least twice: an initial meeting in the first year, and a required practicum readiness consultation. In practice, advising spans four areas.

General advising

  • Support first-semester students with class-flow questions and the stress of being a new student.
  • Send a proactive email or newsletter each semester with important dates (add/drop deadlines) and your office hours.
  • Check in with all advisees at least once per semester, and meet at least twice over the program — an initial first-year meeting and the required practicum readiness consultation.
  • Hold advising in person or on Zoom; a phone message or written note isn't adequate consultation.
  • Keep meetings focused (about 15 minutes) and don't take on more than 40 advisees.

Practicum support

  • Hold pre-practicum conversations about therapy styles and possible clinic site placement.
  • Before the readiness meeting, review the student's course evaluations (aim for scores of 3–4; be ready to discuss any 1–2 ratings) and their unofficial transcript in Colleague.
  • Confirm pre-practicum courses are passed (no incompletes or unretaken fails) and that the student has logged the 20 personal-therapy sessions required before practicum approval (Therapy Verification form).
  • Read through the practicum application together, then sign the form once it's ready — don't just sign it.
  • Point students to the required Practicum 101 workshop (and Practicum 102 after approval), run by the advising & practicum manager.

Problem solving and advocacy

  • First, encourage the student to speak directly with the instructor.
  • If it's unresolved, the faculty advisor is the next step in the grievance process, followed by the co-chairs.
  • Title IX situations: notify the program chairs immediately.
  • Connect students to resources — the Wellness Center, Office of Student Accessibility Services, and Student Affairs.
  • Advocate on other issues (independent study, tuition reimbursement, waitlist anxiety) and keep everyone on track to graduate on time.

Forms & approvals

  • Sign forms as needed: practicum application, change of advisor, fieldwork agreement, and registration forms.
  • Co-sign the leave of absence form (also approved by the co-chairs) and the priority / authorized early registration form.
  • Approve a student's therapist change for the personal-therapy requirement — only one change is allowed.

Student admissions and recruitment

Core faculty take part in admitting and recruiting each new cohort, in cooperation with the Admissions Office.

  • Cooperate with Admissions staff — invite prospective and visiting students into your classroom, and write to or call prospective students.
  • Review applicants for admission and recommend appropriate action, working with the Admissions Office.
  • Help develop the program's recruitment strategy and represent ICP at open houses and information sessions.
  • Support new-student onboarding, including summer registration.
  • Review and score applications in Slate during the admissions cycle.
Confirm the current Slate workflow and admissions-cycle deadlines with your program coordinator.
