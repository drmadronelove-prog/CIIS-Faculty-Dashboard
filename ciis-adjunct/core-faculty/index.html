Advising and recruitment responsibilities

Beyond teaching, core faculty carry two ongoing responsibilities: advising a caseload of ICP students, and helping recruit and admit the next cohort.

Advising

  • Serve as academic advisor to an assigned group of ICP students and check in with each advisee at least once per semester.
  • Help advisees plan their course sequence using the curriculum flow charts and degree requirements.
  • Monitor academic progress and flag incompletes, failed courses, or readiness concerns early.
  • Support practicum readiness and review clinical evaluations as students prepare to apply.
  • Refer students to support services — counseling, accessibility, and basic needs — and to the program chairs for Title IX matters.
  • Keep advising notes and respond to advisee emails promptly using your CIIS email.

Recruitment

  • Review and score applications in Slate during the admissions cycle.
  • Conduct applicant interviews and take part in admissions committee decisions.
  • Represent ICP at open houses, information sessions, and prospective-student events.
  • Support yield — reach out to admitted students and answer their questions about the program.
  • Contribute to program visibility and community partnerships that support enrollment.
For the authoritative version, see the CIIS Faculty Handbook and the ICP Student Handbook.
