Advising

Core faculty serve as academic advisors to ICP students. Advising spans four areas.

General advising

  • Support first-semester students with class-flow questions and the stress of being a new student.
  • Send a proactive email or newsletter each semester with important dates (add/drop deadlines) and your office hours.
  • Check in with all advisees at least once per semester.
  • Limit meetings to 15 minutes.
  • Do not take on more than 40 advisees.

Practicum support

  • Hold pre-practicum conversations about therapy styles and possible clinic site placement.
  • Check students' unofficial transcripts in Colleague — confirm all pre-practicum courses are passed, with no incompletes (I/IP) and no unretaken failed courses.
  • Require students to meet with you in person to go over their practicum application — do not just sign it.
  • Review clinical evaluation forms beforehand to identify themes.
  • Discuss readiness, fears, and anxieties about starting practicum.

Problem solving and advocacy

  • First, encourage the student to speak directly with the instructor.
  • Facilitate a conversation between student and instructor if needed.
  • Title IX situations: notify the program chairs immediately.
  • Advocate for students on other issues — independent study, tuition reimbursement, waitlist anxiety, and more.
  • Keep everyone on track to graduate on time.

Administrative

  • Sign forms as needed: registration, change of advisor, fieldwork agreement, and others.
